THE CURRENT APPROACH TO WASTEWATER MANAGEMENT

The current approach is either onsite or offsite sanitation. ​ Onsite sanitation includes open defecation, Ventilated Improved Pit (VIP) latrine, soap pits, and septic tanks. It poses a health risk pollutes the environment and aggravates social inequality. Offsite sanitation includes conservancy tanks, piped sewerage networks and centralised wastewater treatment works. Offsite sanitation is infrastructure and energy-intensive, is expensive, and requires a high level of skill to build, operate and maintain. ​ Most distribution pipelines are old, resulting in increased water losses. Added to this, high urbanisation trends increase the strain on wastewater treatment works to comply, causing the discharge of large quantities of untreated or partially treated sewage into our water resources.
